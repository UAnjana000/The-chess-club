About Chess Elitist Club

Our Story

Chess Elitist Club was founded in 2020 when five chess enthusiasts realized that playing chess without roasting each other was simply not the vibe.

What started as friendly trash talk over boards has evolved into a full-fledged community where skill is respected, but not without a healthy dose of "did you really just move there?" energy.

Chess Openings We Teach

Learn why your favorite opening is simultaneously brilliant and "literally throwing"

Queen's Gambit

Not just a Netflix show your parents binge-watched. This opening is for those who want to say "I'm sophisticated" without actually saying it.

"Queen's Gambit players have 'live, laugh, love' energy but for chess." - Raghav, probably

Learn when to deploy this opening and when your opponent will simply respond with "nah, I'm good".

Sicilian Defense

The chess equivalent of replying "k" to a paragraph text. Minimal effort from Black that somehow causes maximum frustration for White.

"Playing against the Sicilian is like trying to explain TikTok to your grandparents - confusing and you'll probably fail." - Aryan

We'll teach you all 49372 variations. Well, maybe just the main ones.

London System

The "basic" opening of chess. Set up the same structure every game regardless of what your opponent does. It's giving NPC energy.

"London System players be like: 'I just think it's neat'" - Thulasi

Perfect for when you want to play chess but don't actually want to think too much. No shade. (Full shade.)

Bongcloud Attack

1.e4 e5 2.Ke2. That's it. That's the opening. When you want to disrespect your opponent in the most obvious way possible.

"Playing the Bongcloud is like wearing Crocs to a wedding. Bold statement, questionable choice." - Anjana

We'll teach you when to play this (never) and how to recover from it (you probably can't).
